About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Machine Learning at the Georgia Institute of Technology advised by Tuo Zhao and an ML research intern at Microsoft Research. My research is generously supported by the DoD NDSEG Fellowship.

I received my B.S. in Applied and Computational Mathematics, magna cum laude, at the University of Southern California, where I was a Trustee Scholar and Viterbi Fellow. As an undergraduate researcher in the USC CS Theory Group advised by Shaddin Dughmi, my senior thesis in convex optimization received the Discovery Scholar distinction for exemplary research. I have also been advised by Jason D. Lee and David Kempe. Previously, I was a machine learning research intern at Google X, Sandia National Laboratories and the Air Force Research Laboratory.

I take a scientific approach to reconcile theory with empirical phenomena in deep learning. I develop fast, scrappy experiments that challenge conventional wisdom, then use the results to guide and revise theoretical hypotheses. I am currently focused on topics related to optimization and generalization in deep learning.
