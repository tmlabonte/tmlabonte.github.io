Hey there, I'm Tyler!

I'm a junior at the University of Southern California majoring in Applied and Computational Mathematics with a minor in Computer Science. I am a USC Trustee Scholar and a USC Viterbi Fellow.

I am passionate about tackling the toughest problems in artificial intelligence from a mathematical standpoint. My goal is to develop the theoretical foundations of machine learning, deep learning, and optimization through original, impactful research.

I am currently a Machine Learning Intern at X, the moonshot factory, and I previously did ML work at Sandia National Laboratories and the Air Force Maui Optical and Supercomputing Laboratory.

Check out my most recent paper on Bayesian convolutional neural networks for geometric uncertainty quantification here.

During the fall of 2020, I will be focusing on applying to PhD programs in theoretical machine learning.
